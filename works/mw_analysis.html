Complicity and Collapse in MOUTHWASHING

MOUTHWASHING is a narrative-driven indie game that explores guilt, helplessness, and moral paralysis through fragmented storytelling and the deliberate denial of player agency. Through spatial storytelling, symbolic environments, and limited interaction, the game implicates players in irreversible moral failures, asking them not to actâ€”but to endure. Drawing on theories from Janet Murray, Espen Aarseth, Henry Jenkins, and Abbe Don, my analysis explores how MOUTHWASHING uses the unique properties of digital media to create an experience rooted not in empowerment, but in shared emotional consequence.
