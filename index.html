About Me

I’m a Computer Science and Digital Narrative & Interactive Design student at the University of Pittsburgh, focused on game design. I previously conducted research at Pitt, writing C code for intrusion-tolerant distributed systems, and I enjoy exploring storytelling and interactive systems through games and media. Currently, I’m working on an independent study to design and develop my own game.

Writing

Some info about my writing...

  • Embedded and Emergent: A Framework for Game Narrative Analysis - A framework for analyzing video game narratives by examining both embedded (authored) and emergent (player-created) storytelling, using Papers, Please as a case study.
  • Digital Boundaries and Human Autonomy in Serial Experiments Lain - An analysis of Serial Experiments Lain as a critique of how digital systems blur identity, erode autonomy, and enforce surveillance-driven control.
  • Complicity and Collapse in MOUTHWASHING - An exploration of MOUTHWASHING as a narrative game that denies agency, implicating the player in cycles of guilt, helplessness, and moral collapse.
  • When Rules Write the Story: Exploring Once Upon a Time - A study of how players, rules, and social dynamics shape narratives in the collaborative card game Once Upon a Time.
